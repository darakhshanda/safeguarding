Everyone has the right to feel safe and protected.

Safeguarding

Safeguarding means protecting a person's health, wellbeing, and human rights, enabling them to live free from harm, abuse, and neglect. Learn how one can recognize, prevent, and respond to safeguarding concerns. Recognise the signs, take safe action, and access support.

People need Safeguarding

Children

Children and young people (anyone under 18). Children living in difficult circumstances or facing various threats.

Learn more

Adults at risk

Adults at risk and vulnerable adults; people who have learning disabilities or autism, misuse alcohol or drugs, have a mental health problem or cognitive impairment, lack the mental capacity to protect themselves or make safe decisions.

Learn more

Elderly adults

Elderly people in dependent settings: care homes, hospitals, prisons, schools, supported housing.

Learn more

Anyone at risk

Anyone experiencing coercion, exploitation, trafficking, modern slavery, forced marriage, FGM, socially isolated, homeless, or have language/communication barriers.

Types of Abuse and Neglect

Physical abuse

Physical harm to an individual's body from, for example, hitting, restrictive practices and medication
Signs of neglect include:
  • Bruises, cuts, burns, fractures
  • Fearfulness around certain individuals
  • Flinching when touched
  • Withdrawal from social activities

Domestic abuse

Threats, violence and abuse between individuals that are family members or in an intimate relationship.
Signs of neglect include:
  • Unexplained injuries
  • Changes in behaviour
  • Fearfulness around certain individuals
  • Withdrawal from social activities

Sexual abuse

Sexual relationships or activities that an individual does not or cannot consent to.
Signs of neglect include:
  • Pain or bruising around the inner thigh, anal or breast areas.
  • Pain/discomfort when walking or sitting.
  • Bloodstained underwear.
  • Unexplained STIs/pregnancy

Emotional/psychological abuse

Threats, humiliation, controlling behaviour, blaming, verbal abuse, harassment, intimidation.
Signs of neglect include:
  • Low self-worth
  • Changes in mood or behaviour
  • Withdrawal from social activities
  • Fearfulness around certain individuals

Use of an individual's money or possessions without permission. Includes theft, fraud, scamming, coercion, self-serving involvement in an individual's financial affairs.
Signs of neglect include:
  • Unpaid bills
  • Lack of money for basic necessities
  • Missing possessions or monies
  • Poor living conditions

Modern slavery

Individuals being forced into a life of slavery, human trafficking, domestic servitude or forced labour.
Signs of neglect include:
  • Signs of physical or emotional abuse
  • Malnutrition or dehydration
  • Poor living conditions
  • Few or no personal possessions

Discriminatory abuse

Individuals being treated differently due to personal traits such as age, gender, race, sexual orientation etc.
Signs of neglect include:
  • Unequal treatment
  • Use of derogatory language
  • Social isolation
  • Harassment or bullying

Institutional/organisational abuse

When an organisation's needs are put above an individual's needs e.g. telling an individual that they have to go to bed at a certain time
Signs of neglect include:
  • Inflexibility
  • Poor staff knowledge and training
  • Non person-centred approaches
  • Poor standards

Neglect by others

People responsible for an individual not seeing to their basic needs such as nutrition or hygiene either deliberately or inadvertently.
Signs of neglect include:
  • Malnutrition
  • Dehydration
  • Dirty clothes/bedding
  • Poor hygiene
  • Taking medication incorrectly
  • Bedsores
