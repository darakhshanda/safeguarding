Safeguarding

Safeguarding means protecting a person's health, wellbeing, and human rights, enabling them to live free from harm, abuse, and neglect. Learn how one can recognize, prevent, and respond to safeguarding concerns. Recognise the signs, take safe action, and access support.

6 Principles of Safeguarding

List of Principles
Empowerment: People are supported and encouraged to make their own decisions, giving informed consent whenever possible.
Prevention: Actions are taken before harm occurs to stop abuse, neglect, or exploitation from happening in the first place.
Proportionality: Responses to risks are appropriate and the least intrusive necessary based on the threat or situation.
Protection: Support and representation are given to those most at risk, ensuring that people are kept safe from harm. Partnership: Organisations and local communities work together, sharing information and resources to detect and prevent neglect and abuse.
Accountability: All safeguarding activities are transparent and everyone involved is responsible for ensuring best practice standards are met.
These principles underpin safeguarding policies in health and social care across the UK, aiming to ensure that vulnerable individuals are protected and respected.
